Basic Bunny Facts

  • Adult rabbits usually weigh between 2.4 and 5.5 pounds.
  • They can jump over 2 feet high in a single leap.
  • Pet rabbits typically live 5–10 years, and even longer if spayed or neutered.
  • Baby rabbits are called kits or kittens and are born blind and hairless, relying completely on their mother for warmth and safety.
  • Mother rabbits can give birth to as many as 14 kits in one litter, after a pregnancy lasting just 28–31 days.

Rabbit Bodies & Senses

  • Rabbits’ eyes are located on the sides of their head, allowing them nearly 360° vision to watch for predators.
  • They have large ears that rotate independently to detect sounds and help regulate body temperature by releasing excess heat.
  • Rabbit teeth grow continuously throughout their life but are naturally worn down by chewing hay, grass, and vegetables.
  • A rabbit's top front teeth can grow about 3mm per week if not kept in check by proper chewing.

Behaviors & Movements

  • Rabbits can purr by softly grinding their teeth- it’s a sign they’re happy and content.
  • When feeling playful or joyful, they may perform a "binky"- a jump with a twist and kick in midair!
  • Loafing, nudging, grooming, and chin- rubbing are other common rabbit behaviors that express comfort or curiosity.
  • Despite their small size, rabbits can reach speeds up to 35 miles per hour when running!

Poop, Digestion & Health

  • Rabbits produce two types of droppings: regular round pellets and cecotropes- nutrient-rich droppings that they eat to support digestion and overall health.
  • Staying hydrated and eating lots of hay is essential for their gut health and to prevent dental issues.

Social Life & Friendships

  • Rabbits are social animals and can form close bonds with other rabbits and sometimes with other pets like guinea pigs or dogs- under careful supervision.
  • They communicate through body language, subtle sounds, and scent marking, making them both unique and expressive companions.
