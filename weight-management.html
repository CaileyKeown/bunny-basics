Managing Your Bunny's Weight

Keeping your bunny at a healthy weight is vital for a long, comfortable life. Overweight rabbits may develop heart, liver, or digestive issues, while underweight bunnies could face malnutrition, weak immunity, and poor energy. Monitoring their weight regularly is a key part of good rabbit care.

How to Monitor Your Bunny's Weight

  • Weigh regularly: Weigh your rabbit monthly with a digital scale for early signs of weight gain or loss.
  • Use the Body Condition Score (BCS): A 1–5 scale helps you feel for bones and fat layers to judge if your rabbit is underweight, ideal, or overweight.

Body Condition Score Chart

  • 1: Emaciated – Bones sharp and visible.
  • 2: Thin – Ribs and spine easily felt.
  • 3: Ideal – Ribs are felt with slight pressure.
  • 4: Overweight – Fat deposits noticeable; ribs hard to feel.
  • 5: Obese – Thick fat layer; ribs and spine not felt.

Signs and Causes of Obesity

Fat rolls around the dewlap or belly, sluggish movement, and fast weight gain are all red flags. Common causes include too many sugary treats, a pellet-heavy diet, and not enough exercise or space to hop around.

Helping an Overweight Rabbit

  • Adjust their diet: Feed more hay and leafy greens, cut back on high-calcium pellets and sugary snacks.
  • Encourage movement: Toys, tunnels, and free-roam time boost activity and burn calories.
  • Vet visits: Get tailored advice and monitor their weight loss safely. Rapid weight loss is dangerous.

Identifying an Underweight Bunny

  • Visible bones: You can feel or see the ribs, spine, and hips.
  • Low energy: A lack of energy, dull fur, or reduced appetite may point to weight loss or illness.

Helping an Underweight Rabbit

  • Boost calories: Add more hay, dark greens, and high-quality pellets to meals.
  • Check for health issues: Parasites, dental issues, or stress may be the cause.
  • Track progress: Weigh them weekly and consult your vet for nutrition plans.

Smart Feeding for a Healthy Bunny

  • Hay: Should be the base of every bunny’s diet. It supports digestion and dental health.
  • Greens: Offer a variety like kale, romaine, or herbs. Avoid high-calcium greens in large amounts.
  • Pellets: Give in moderation. Choose high-fiber, low-sugar options.
  • Water: Keep it clean and always available to help with digestion and hydration.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too many treats: Stick to healthy snacks like herbs or small fruit bits.
  • Too little movement: Exercise is just as important as diet.
  • Ignoring sudden changes: Weight gain or loss could mean health issues – contact your vet.
